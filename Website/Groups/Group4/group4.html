Everyday Life in Plano Piloto
The plan according to which the Plano Piloto was built didn't only include positions of buildings and roads. It focused on how people would be using the created places as well. The plan included a detailed structute on where and how the inhabitans were supposed to design their every day life.

Supply Offer

The following section will describe how the supply structure was originally planned and how it looks like today. Let's see, how the supply offer has changed.

How was the local supply planned?

In contrast to the largely unplanned settlements of the satellite towns, in the Plano Piloto, especially in Asa Sul and Asa Norte, there were precise plans for the local supply of the inhabitants. In the so-called "superquadras"(SQ), or rather in the communities of four superquadras “Unidade de Vizinhança” (UdV), there were schools, medical facilities, playgrounds, clubs, swimming pools, gyms, as well as small stores with local supply possibilities (Madanelo 1996, p. 276). Most of the supply was to be covered by small local commercial areas with, for example, bakeries and butcher shops (Tattara 2011, p. 52ff.) These are located in the “Comércio Local” (CL). A CL is always located between the two adjacent SQ. A very closely related theory is Walter Christaller's theory of central places. This theory deals with consumption and spatial planning. Basically, the theory describes how area-wide supply of the population with goods can be implemented in the shortest possible distances. It explains how supply centers are formed and how they relate to each other. The theory is based on the homo economicus approach. According to this, a person always possesses all the information and decides in favor of the offer that makes the most sense (Popp 2020, p.76).

What does local supply look like today?

Compared to the 1960s, when the life in Plano Piloto began, society and especially consumer behavior has changed drastically. Even in Plano Piloto, today's supply structures look different to some extent. In order to determine the current local supply structures, the Method of mapping was chosen. With this method, current structures of the local supply system could be traced. The evaluations of the mapping show that the CLs are still present. In addition, there are shopping centers that are used by the residents. The CLs differed from each other in their specialization. Some CLs have a focus on clothing, specialty items or restaurants, while others are largely heterogeneous. The analysis of UdV 107/307 & 108/308 Sul, for example, shows that Clothing and Other retail predominates (see the figure below) and in restaurants predominate in UdV 202/402 & 203/403 Norte. What has changed? Interpretation

In summary, it can be noted that the local supply system has changed a lot, but in Plano Piloto the basic structures have been preserved until today. CL can still be seen as supply centers, following the central places theory, with the shopping centers increasingly competing. The range of services offered within the CLs varies greatly, but basic services are still available in each of the CLs we studied. It was also noticeable that there was no economic disparity in more distant CLs. Vacancies could be observed everywhere. In conclusion, the supply offer in the Plano Piloto has increased, specialized in some places and is increasingly controlled by the shopping centers. In addition, the importance of supermarkets, which now bundle most of the local supply, has changed considerably. Every UdV has at least one large supermarket. Here, the results of our analysis overlap with those of Bezerra and Agner (2021). Following our hypotheses, it can be stated that a long-term planning of the local supply with a general master plan is not possible and is strongly influenced by social change. Furthermore, it has been shown that the supply structure within Destrito Federal is very heterogeneous. See here the supply in the satellite town of Aguas Claras (link).

Data collection

Besides looking at the development of the supply structure in the Plano Piloto, a second focus was set on the demand inhabitants concerning main supply. This includes for example where and how inhabitants of the Plano Piloto are going grocery shopping, having lunch, and doing other basic supply activities nowadays. Therefore, two different methods were practiced:
questionnaires The questionnaires were handed out to students of the UNB who live in Plano Piloto such as random pedestrians we’ve met while mapping the CLs. The total number of filled out questionnaires is six, three men and three women in the age between 23 and 65. The questionnaire contained five open questions, one drawing section for a mental map ending with some demographic questions about age, gender, living situation, number of people living in the same apartment and if they are working and/or studying. This method could provide an overview of different opinions and practices. Anyways, with only six people answering in total, we can’t take any conclusions for general habits out of the results. In order to still use the filled-out questionnaires, all answers were collected and sorted into a table which provides an overview of different habits concerning each topic of the questions asked. The mental maps were analyzed and compared. Some of them are displayed in the following section.
and
trackingsThe trackings were practiced by four people living in Plano Piloto. Three male and one female between 23 and 35 years old. They were given an account for an app called Strava. With this app, people are usually tracking their sport activities (e.g., for running: route, distance, speed, elevation, heart rate). For this study the focus was only on the route. After setting up their smartphone with the Strava app and given account, they started a tracking activity on Strava but continued their everyday life just as they would without tracking. The tracking was active for four days with breaks at night. The participants could also stop and start the tracking at any time and any place if they wanted to. These trackings resulted into exportable routes about the movement of the tracked people. Like this it’s possible to see, if they go to the same CL every day, to different CLs around their SQ and further away, if they leave Plano Piloto, if they stop at a supermarket while on their way from A to B, etc. The following map shows the different tracks of the four participants during their four days of tracking.

Even in the small number of participants, a wide variety of locations can be determined. This includes the mentions of local organic markets, organic weekly fairs, bakeries, shops in the nearby CL, supermarkets and even wholesale. As answers to the question, how they would choose the location for grocery shopping, the participants mentioned the quality, price, availability of parking spots and short distances to their apartment. Another questions was asking where the participants are eating their main meals. The answers are ranging from eating at home, cooking at home and taking it with them during the day to fast food, eating at restaurants depending on the day and using the cafeteria at university. As a fourth question, the relevance of ordering food online was queried. One of the participants stated that ordering food online is very important. The other answers stated a lower relevance with statements like “only if time is short”, “only if money is left” or “only out of convenience”. By drawing a mental map, the participants were asked to display where they are buying their supplies in relation to their home, how they move between shopping and home as well as how much time it takes them to get to different places.
